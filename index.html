Nablus

Nablus, a city in northern Palestine, is one of the country's oldest and most historically significant urban centers. Nestled between Mount Ebal and Mount Gerizim, it has a rich cultural heritage dating back over 2,000 years. Founded as the Roman city of Neapolis, Nablus has seen various civilizations, from the Canaanites and Romans to the Ottomans and modern Palestinians.

The city is renowned for its bustling markets, soap production, and signature sweet, Knafeh. Nablus is also home to historic sites such as the Great Mosque, Jacob's Well, and the ancient Samaritan community on Mount Gerizim. Today, it serves as a cultural and economic hub, combining tradition with modernity, and plays a vital role in Palestinian society.
